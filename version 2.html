The Human Cost of Unaffordable Eye Care

Every day, millions of Americans suffer from the burning, itching, and blurred vision of dry eye syndrome. For many, a small bottle of eye drops is the only hope for relief. Yet, despite costing just pennies to produce, these 15ml bottles are sold for $16, putting much-needed relief out of reach.

According to recent surveys, approximately 48% of dry eye sufferers report skipping or rationing their eye drops due to cost concerns. When left untreated, dry eye syndrome can cause the surface of the cornea to deteriorate, leading to painful, persistent ulcers and infections. Over time, this damage can trigger extensive scarring and inflammation, which can lead to permanent vision loss or blindness.

The Shocking Reality of Eye Drop Pricing

The price you pay for eye drops has almost nothing to do with what they cost to make. A 15ml bottle of Refresh Tears retails for around $16, while the actual ingredients cost approximately $0.002 — just two-tenths of a penny.

The Markup Reality

Comparing the manufacturing cost ($0.002) to the retail price ($16) reveals a markup of 800,000%. Even with additional costs like packaging and distribution, this margin is extraordinary.

For perspective, insulin production costs around $2–$4 while retail prices exceed $250, representing a markup of 6,000–12,000%.

Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about eye drops and affordable alternatives.

Why are eye drops so expensive?

Eye drops have high prices due to several factors including pharmaceutical companies' marketing costs, research and development investments, and pricing strategies designed to maximize profit. However, the actual manufacturing cost is extremely low compared to the retail price, suggesting that pricing is primarily driven by market factors rather than production costs.

Are generic eye drops as effective as brand name?

Yes, generic eye drops with the same active ingredients as brand-name products are generally equally effective. The FDA requires generic medications to have the same active ingredients, strength, and dosage form as their brand-name counterparts. Always check the active ingredient list to ensure you're getting the appropriate type for your condition.

How can I make my eye drops last longer?

To extend the life of your eye drops:

  • Store them properly according to package instructions
  • Be careful not to touch the tip to avoid contamination
  • Use only the recommended dosage
  • Try preservative-free single-dose units if you only need occasional relief
  • Consider environmental factors that may be contributing to dry eyes, such as screen time, dry air, or ceiling fans
What are natural alternatives to eye drops?

While not always as immediately effective as commercial eye drops, some natural approaches to eye comfort include:

  • Staying well-hydrated throughout the day
  • Using a humidifier in dry environments
  • Taking regular breaks from screens (follow the 20-20-20 rule: every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds)
  • Applying warm compresses for MGD (Meibomian Gland Dysfunction)
  • Consuming omega-3 fatty acids, which may help with tear production

Always consult with your eye care professional before replacing prescribed treatments.
